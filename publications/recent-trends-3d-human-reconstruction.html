Survey on 3D Human Body Reconstruction Techniques

Summer Annual Conference of IEIE2023

This paper reviews recent trends in 3D human reconstruction and analyzes how different approaches contribute to understanding human shape and structure from visual data. It provides an overview of the field and highlights important directions in human-centered computer vision research.

Citation

Paper Overview

The purpose of this paper is to organize and analyze recent research trends in 3D human reconstruction. As computer vision and deep learning techniques have advanced, various methods have been proposed to reconstruct human shape, pose, and appearance from 2D or 3D inputs. This paper examines those approaches from a broader perspective.

By reviewing the literature, the paper provides a structured understanding of the development of 3D human reconstruction techniques and helps clarify how the field is evolving toward more detailed and practical human-centered applications.

Review Summary

  • Surveyed recent approaches to 3D human reconstruction
  • Compared how different methods model human shape, pose, and appearance
  • Analyzed the role of computer vision and deep learning in the field
  • Highlighted the research direction of human-centered 3D understanding

Key Contributions

  • Provided an organized review of recent 3D human reconstruction research
  • Helped clarify major methodological trends in the field
  • Contributed to understanding how human-centered 3D reconstruction is developing
  • Built a foundational perspective for later research in 3D human analysis
