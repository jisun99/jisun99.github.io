Two-stage Anthropometric Landmark Detection Framework on Human Point Cloud: Dynamic-invariant and Static-specific Strategy

Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence Accepted · 2026

This paper proposes a two-stage framework for anthropometric landmark detection on 3D human point clouds. The proposed method is designed to preserve landmark consistency under dynamic posture variation through a shape-pose invariant stage and to further improve point-level precision on the original input surface through a point-specific refinement stage. By combining robustness and precision within a unified framework, the work aims to make landmark detection more reliable for practical 3D human body analysis.

Journal Page Citation

Research Motivation

Anthropometric landmark detection is a fundamental task for human body measurement, ergonomic analysis, and digital human understanding. However, existing approaches often face a trade-off between robustness and precision. Methods operating directly on the input point cloud may achieve locally accurate predictions, but they are often sensitive to posture variation and geometric deformation. In contrast, methods relying on a parametric human template can offer more stable anatomical correspondence, yet they do not directly provide the final landmark position on the original input surface.

This work was motivated by the need to bridge these two requirements. The goal was not only to detect landmarks accurately, but also to preserve anatomical consistency across dynamic postures and then refine the prediction back onto the input point cloud. In this sense, the proposed method was designed to connect dynamic-invariant landmark estimation with static-specific geometric refinement in a single end-to-end framework.

Why a two-stage strategy was necessary

A single-stage formulation was not sufficient to fully address both posture robustness and point-level precision. If the model directly predicts landmarks only on the input point cloud, the prediction can become unstable under large posture deformation. On the other hand, if the method predicts landmarks only on the parametric template, the resulting landmark is anatomically meaningful but does not exactly correspond to the original scan surface. The two-stage design was therefore introduced to explicitly separate these two objectives while preserving their connection.

Core idea of the paper

  • Stage 1 establishes posture-consistent landmark estimation on a parametric human template.
  • Stage 2 transfers and refines those landmarks directly on the input point cloud.
  • The overall framework links dynamic-invariant estimation with static-specific precision.
  • The method enables both landmark consistency across postures and more accurate localization on the real surface.

Proposed Framework

The overall framework consists of two stages: shape-pose invariant landmark detection (SI-LD) for consistent landmark estimation on the parametric template, followed by point-specific landmark detection (PI-LD) for refined prediction directly on the input point cloud.

Stage 1: Shape-pose Invariant Landmark Detection (SI-LD)

The first stage is designed to estimate anatomically consistent landmarks under posture variation. Instead of working directly on the raw input point cloud, the input scan is first associated with a parametric human template representation. Landmark estimation is then performed in this template domain, where consistent vertex correspondence helps preserve landmark identity even when the body shape or pose changes.

This stage plays a critical role in the overall framework because it provides a stable landmark reference that is less sensitive to deformation. In other words, SI-LD is responsible for the dynamic-invariant characteristic of the proposed method. It allows the same anatomical landmark concept to be maintained across different postures, which is essential for practical anthropometric analysis in non-static settings.

Why heatmap-based landmark prediction was used

Rather than directly regressing landmark coordinates, the method adopts a heatmap-based formulation. This design better preserves spatial structure and reduces the instability that can arise when multiple plausible surface candidates exist near the target region. In this work, the target heatmap is defined using geodesic distance on the surface so that the landmark representation better reflects intrinsic body geometry even under posture variation.

Role of SI-LD
  • Provides posture-robust landmark estimation
  • Maintains anatomical consistency across deformation
  • Acts as the dynamic-invariant stage of the framework
Key Characteristics
  • Template-based landmark reasoning
  • Heatmap prediction on structured surface geometry
  • Stable initialization for the second stage

Stage 2: Point-specific Landmark Detection (PI-LD)

While SI-LD provides stable anatomical landmark estimation, its output is still defined in the template domain rather than directly on the original point cloud. The second stage is therefore introduced to recover surface-specific precision. Based on the landmark estimated in Stage 1, the input point cloud is locally segmented around the corresponding region, and landmark prediction is refined directly on the input data.

This stage is responsible for the static-specific characteristic of the proposed method. Its purpose is to improve point-level localization by focusing on the actual geometry of the input scan, which is often much denser and more detailed than the parametric template. As a result, PI-LD complements SI-LD by translating stable landmark identity into more precise landmark positioning on the real surface.

Why this refinement matters

  • The parametric template preserves correspondence but has limited geometric resolution.
  • The original input point cloud contains finer local surface detail.
  • Refinement around the predicted region improves actual landmark accuracy on the scan.
  • This stage links anatomical consistency with real-surface precision.
Role of PI-LD
  • Refines landmarks on the original point cloud
  • Improves local geometric precision
  • Acts as the static-specific stage of the framework
Key Characteristics
  • Segment-based local refinement
  • Uses SI-LD prediction as a reliable guide

ALTNet and Geodesic Heatmap Design

A key component of the proposed framework is ALTNet, a feature extraction network designed to better capture landmark-oriented geometry. ALTNet extends global-local point cloud representation by explicitly emphasizing the regions surrounding landmarks, which is particularly important for anthropometric landmark detection where subtle local geometric cues matter.

In addition, the method uses geodesic heatmap representation rather than simple Euclidean-distance-based labeling. This design is important because human body surfaces undergo non-rigid deformation under pose changes, and geodesic distance better reflects intrinsic surface relationships than straight-line Euclidean distance. Together, ALTNet and geodesic heatmap learning support both the stability of SI-LD and the precision of PI-LD.

Qualitative and Quantitative Results

The proposed framework was designed to show two complementary advantages: consistent landmark prediction under posture variation and improved surface-level precision after local refinement. These two properties are jointly demonstrated through the SI-LD and PI-LD stages.

What the results show

The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed framework is effective not simply because it improves a single accuracy number, but because it addresses two different but related requirements of anthropometric landmark detection. First, SI-LD enables stable landmark estimation under posture variation, making it possible to preserve consistent anatomical correspondence. Second, PI-LD improves the final localization on the input point cloud, which is essential when the goal is to obtain precise landmarks on the actual scan surface.

This distinction is important because the paper is not only about outperforming benchmark models in a narrow sense. It is about presenting a more practically useful formulation for 3D landmark detection, especially in scenarios where posture variation and geometric precision must both be considered.

Main contributions of the paper

  • Proposed a two-stage landmark detection framework that explicitly separates invariant estimation and precise refinement.
  • Introduced SI-LD to preserve landmark consistency under dynamic posture variation.
  • Introduced PI-LD to refine landmarks directly on the original point cloud for improved precision.
  • Applied ALTNet and geodesic heatmap representation to better support anthropometric landmark reasoning.
  • Demonstrated the practical potential of the method for reliable 3D human body analysis.
Why It Matters

The proposed method is meaningful because it does not force practitioners to choose between posture robustness and point-level precision. Instead, it provides a structured way to obtain both through a unified framework.

Practical Value
