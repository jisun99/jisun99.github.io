About

A Vision AI researcher connecting practical engineering with academic research across 2D and 3D domains

I work in Vision AI based on industrial experience in 2D object detection and academic research experience in 3D point cloud processing and anthropometric landmark detection.

Currently, I am a Senior AI Researcher at HD Korea Shipbuilding & Offshore Engineering, where I develop and improve industrial Vision AI models for real-world environments.

At the same time, my research has focused on 3D human scan data, including landmark detection, dynamic posture-robust estimation, and point-level refinement. I am especially interested in how human-centered computer vision technologies can be applied in practical settings.

Research

Research interests spanning from 2D perception to 3D human understanding

2D Object Detection

I am interested in developing object detection models for practical industrial environments and improving robust visual perception under real deployment conditions.

3D Point Cloud Processing

I study representation learning and geometric analysis for 3D human data, with a focus on effectively capturing both structural information and local features from point clouds.

Human Landmark Detection

My research focuses on deep learning-based methods for precise anatomical landmark detection on 3D human scan data.

Deep Learning for Vision AI

I am interested in designing practical AI systems that can operate reliably across different data modalities and real-world problem settings.
